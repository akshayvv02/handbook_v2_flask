Florida Cybersecurity Statutes and Laws

The state of Florida has implemented several statutes and laws to enhance cybersecurity and protect sensitive information. Some key statutes include:

F.S. 282.3185 - Local Government Cybersecurity Act

The Local Government Cybersecurity Act mandates cybersecurity standards and reporting requirements for local governments in Florida. It emphasizes the need for local governments to adopt cybersecurity best practices, conduct regular risk assessments, and report cybersecurity incidents to the appropriate state authorities.

  • Requires local governments to establish and implement cybersecurity programs.
  • Mandates regular cybersecurity training for employees.
  • Requires reporting of cybersecurity incidents to the Florida Digital Service.

Local Government Cybersecurity Act (F.S. 282.3185)

F.S. 501.171 - Florida Information Protection Act (FIPA)

The Florida Information Protection Act requires businesses and government entities to protect personal information and notify individuals of data breaches. It outlines specific measures for safeguarding data and sets timelines for breach notifications.

  • Requires reasonable measures to protect personal information.
  • Mandates breach notification to affected individuals within 30 days.
  • Imposes penalties for non-compliance.

Florida Information Protection Act (FIPA) (F.S. 501.171)

F.S. 668.50 - Uniform Electronic Transaction Act

The Uniform Electronic Transaction Act provides a legal framework for secure electronic transactions and signatures in Florida. It ensures that electronic records and signatures are as legally binding as their paper counterparts.

  • Recognizes electronic signatures and records as legally valid.
  • Facilitates electronic transactions between parties.
  • Ensures the security and integrity of electronic records.

Uniform Electronic Transaction Act (F.S. 668.50)

F.S. 282.0041 - State Technology Office and Cybersecurity Responsibilities

This statute defines the responsibilities of the State Technology Office regarding cybersecurity. It outlines the office's duties in developing and implementing cybersecurity policies, procedures, and standards for state agencies.

  • Establishes the State Technology Office's role in cybersecurity.
  • Mandates the development of cybersecurity policies and procedures.
  • Requires state agencies to comply with cybersecurity standards.

State Technology Office and Cybersecurity Responsibilities (F.S. 282.0041)

F.S. 282.318 - Enterprise Security of Data and Information Technology

This statute requires state agencies to establish and maintain an enterprise cybersecurity program. It emphasizes the importance of protecting state data and information technology resources from unauthorized access and other cyber threats.

  • Mandates enterprise cybersecurity programs for state agencies.
  • Requires regular security assessments and audits.
  • Establishes incident response protocols.

Enterprise Security of Data and Information Technology (F.S. 282.318)

F.S. 282.319 - Florida Cybersecurity Governance Act

This statute establishes the governance framework for cybersecurity in Florida. It outlines the roles and responsibilities of various state entities in maintaining and improving the state's cybersecurity posture.

  • Establishes the Florida Cybersecurity Advisory Council.
  • Mandates the development of a statewide cybersecurity strategy.
  • Requires state agencies to report cybersecurity incidents.

Florida Cybersecurity Governance Act (F.S. 282.319)

F.S. 1004.444 - Florida Center for Cybersecurity

This statute establishes the Florida Center for Cybersecurity at the University of South Florida. The center aims to enhance cybersecurity education, research, and outreach across the state.

  • Promotes cybersecurity education and workforce development.
  • Facilitates research and innovation in cybersecurity.
  • Engages in public outreach to raise cybersecurity awareness.

Florida Center for Cybersecurity (F.S. 1004.444)
