Ransomware

Ransomware is a type of malicious software designed to block access to a computer system or data, usually by encrypting it, until a ransom is paid. Attackers typically demand payment in cryptocurrency, making it difficult to trace. Ransomware can spread through phishing emails, malicious websites, or by exploiting vulnerabilities in software.


"You receive an email with an urgent request. Should you click the link?"

How Ransomware Works

Ransomware is a cyber attack that locks your files or system until a ransom is paid. It often spreads through deceptive emails, compromised websites, or vulnerabilities in your software. The attackers demand payment, typically in cryptocurrency, making it hard to track them down.


It infiltrates your system

The ransomware gains access through phishing emails or by exploiting unpatched vulnerabilities. Once inside, it encrypts your files or locks you out of your system.


It demands payment

After locking you out, the ransomware displays a ransom note, typically demanding payment in cryptocurrency, with the promise of decrypting your data.


It threatens your business

Failure to pay could result in permanent data loss, leading to significant financial and reputational damage to your business.



What You Can Do

Before you become a victim of a ransomware attack, take these preventive measures:


  • Backup regularly: Regularly back up your critical data and store the backups offline. This ensures you can recover your data without paying the ransom.
  • Exercise caution with emails: Be wary of unsolicited emails, especially those with attachments or links. Verify the sender’s identity before interacting with the content.
  • Update software: Keep your operating system, applications, and security software up to date with the latest patches to minimize the risk of ransomware exploitation.

How to Protect Your Business

Implement these strategies to safeguard your business from ransomware:


  • Employee training: Educate your employees on recognizing phishing emails and other ransomware delivery methods.
  • Implement security measures: Use robust security solutions like firewalls, antivirus software, and intrusion detection systems to protect your network.
  • Network segmentation: Segment your network to limit ransomware spread, preventing it from affecting your entire system.
  • Access controls: Implement strict access controls to reduce the risk of ransomware spreading through your network.

What if You Fall for a Ransomware Attack

If you become a victim of a ransomware attack, follow these steps:


1. Isolate infected systems:

Immediately disconnect infected systems from the network to prevent the ransomware from spreading.

2. Report the incident:

Notify your IT department and follow your organization’s incident response plan. Early reporting helps contain the damage.

3. Do not pay the ransom:

Paying the ransom does not guarantee that you will regain access to your data. Focus on recovering data from backups.

4. Engage professionals:

Contact cybersecurity experts to assist in removing the ransomware and restoring your systems.

5. Notify affected parties:

If sensitive data was compromised, notify the relevant stakeholders and provide them with guidance on protecting themselves.
